Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Early Life

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He came from a modest background and his parents were deeply religious. Despite financial difficulties, Abdul Kalam was a bright student with a passion for learning.

Education

Kalam graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later pursued aerospace engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur.

Career

Dr. Kalam started his career in the defense sector at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He played a pivotal role in India’s space and missile programs, contributing to projects like the launch of the satellite Rohini and the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Presidency

In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. He was popularly known as the "People's President" due to his humble nature and approachable demeanor.

Later Years and Legacy

After his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to inspire young minds through his lectures and writings. He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds". He was awarded numerous accolades for his contributions to science and education, including the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award.

Death

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His legacy lives on as an inspiration to millions of people across India and the world.
